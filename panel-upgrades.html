The Heart of Your Home's Power

As homes in the DMV area evolve, so do their power requirements. Older homes, particularly historic properties in Frederick, MD, or classic rowhouses in Washington D.C., were often built with 60-amp or 100-amp electrical panels. Today, modern homes require a minimum of 200-amp service (often referred to regionally as a "heavy-up"). Hover or tap to reveal warning signs.

Tripping Breakers

If you frequently have to reset breakers when running the microwave and vacuum simultaneously, your current system is severely overloaded.

Flickering Lights

Dimming or flickering lights when large appliances (like AC units or sump pumps) kick on indicate an unstable, insufficient power flow.

Recalled Brands

Federal Pacific (FPE) or Zinsco panels are known, documented fire hazards that fail to trip during overloads. They must be replaced.

Out of Space

If your breaker box is full and you are planning to add an EV charger, hot tub, or remodel a kitchen, you will need a panel upgrade to make room.

Are You Planning a Remodel?

Adding a high-end kitchen, finishing a basement, or installing an induction stove or an electric vehicle (EV) charging station almost always requires a panel heavy-up. We perform strict load calculations to ensure your new additions have the power they safely need.
