Why Do You Need a Panel Upgrade?

As homes in the DMV area evolve, so do their power requirements. Older homes, particularly historic properties in Frederick, MD, or classic rowhouses in Washington D.C., were often built with 60-amp or 100-amp electrical panels. Today, between massive HVAC systems, smart appliances, home theaters, and Level 2 EV chargers, modern homes require a minimum of 200-amp service (often referred to regionally as a "heavy-up").

Signs It's Time to Upgrade Your Electrical Panel

  • Tripping Breakers: If you frequently have to reset breakers when running the microwave and vacuum simultaneously, your system is overloaded.
  • Flickering Lights: Dimming or flickering lights when large appliances (like AC units or sump pumps) kick on indicate an unstable, insufficient power flow.
  • Adding High-Draw Appliances: Remodeling a kitchen, finishing a basement, or adding a hot tub, induction stove, or an electric vehicle (EV) charging station almost always requires a panel heavy-up.
  • Outdated or Recalled Panels: If your home has a Federal Pacific Electric (FPE), Zinsco, or older fuse box, these are known, documented fire hazards that fail to trip during overloads. They should be replaced immediately.
