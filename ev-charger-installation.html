Faster, Safer At-Home Charging

Relying on a standard 120-volt wall outlet (Level 1) can take days to fully charge a modern electric vehicle. For DMV commuters, a Level 2 Charging Station (240-volt) is essential, delivering up to 40 miles of range per hour of charge.

Eminent Power Solutions is experienced in hardwiring and high-capacity outlet installations (NEMA 14-50) for all major EV brands. We regularly install Tesla Wall Connectors, ChargePoint Home Flex, Rivian Wall Chargers, and Ford Charge Station Pro systems.

The EPS Installation Process

  • Load Calculation: We assess your home's current electrical panel to ensure it can safely handle the sustained high amperage (typically 40 to 60 amps) required by a Level 2 charger.
  • Dedicated Circuitry: We install a dedicated circuit directly from your breaker box to your garage or driveway, ensuring no other appliances interfere with your charging speed.
  • Code Compliance: We use the exact proper wiring gauge and conduit installation to meet strict local Maryland and Virginia electrical codes, completely eliminating fire hazards associated with DIY or sub-par installs.
  • Heavy-Up Integration: If your older D.C. or Frederick home lacks the capacity, we can bundle your EV charger installation with a seamless panel heavy-up.

EV Charging FAQs

It depends on your current capacity. If you have an older 100-amp panel and run an electric stove, dryer, and HVAC, adding a 50-amp EV charger will likely overload your system, requiring a heavy-up to 200 amps. If you already have 200-amp service, you are usually perfectly fine. We perform a strict load calculation before any install to tell you exactly what is required.

Hardwiring is generally safer, looks cleaner, and allows for faster charging speeds (up to 48 or 60 amps depending on the unit). A NEMA 14-50 plug-in setup maxes out at 40 amps of charging speed but gives you the flexibility to easily unplug the unit and take it with you if you move.
