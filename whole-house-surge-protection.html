Why Power Strips Aren't Enough

Standard plug-in surge protectors only defend the items plugged directly into them, and their internal components degrade silently over time. They offer zero protection for your $8,000 heat pump or your smart refrigerator. We install commercial-grade Surge Protective Devices (SPDs) directly into your main electrical panel to create an umbrella of protection over the entire house.

Surge Protection FAQs

No consumer-grade surge protector can withstand a 100% direct lightning strike to your home's physical structure. However, it will aggressively defend against the incredibly common surges caused when lightning strikes a nearby power pole, sending massive voltage spikes down the utility lines.

Surge protectors are categorized by where they are installed in your electrical system. We utilize a "cascading" method to provide maximum defense.

SPD ClassificationInstall LocationPrimary Function
Type 1 SPDUtility MeterBlocks massive external utility & lightning surges
Type 2 SPDMain Breaker PanelHandles both external grid spikes and internal motor surges
Type 3 SPDWall Receptacle (Plug-in)Point-of-use strips to catch minor residual electrical noise
Type 4 SPDInternal ComponentsFactory-installed protection inside individual appliances

Most high-quality SPDs will last 3 to 5 years, depending on the frequency of electrical surges in your area. They act like a sponge, absorbing excess voltage until their internal MOVs are "full." The units we install feature green LED indicator lights so you know when they need replacement.
