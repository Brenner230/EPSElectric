Do They Really Expire?

Yes. The internal sensors in smoke and carbon monoxide detectors degrade over time due to dust accumulation and chemical breakdown. The NFPA dictates that all smoke detectors must be replaced 10 years from the date of manufacture (printed on the back), and CO detectors every 7 to 10 years. If your alarms have yellowed plastic, they are expired and no longer reliable.

Life-Safety FAQs

Even hardwired detectors have battery backups to work during power outages. When that 9V battery runs low, the unit emits a warning chirp. Drops in nighttime temperatures reduce battery voltage, which is why the chirping almost always starts in the middle of the night. We solve this by installing modern 10-year sealed lithium backup models.
