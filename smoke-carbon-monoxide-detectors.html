The Flaw with Battery-Only Detectors

Standalone battery detectors have a critical flaw: if a fire starts in the basement at 2:00 AM, the basement alarm will sound, but you might not hear it in your second-story bedroom until it's too late. To maximize reaction time, modern safety standards require interconnected detector systems.

The Benefits of a Hardwired System

  • Interconnectivity: When one alarm senses smoke or CO, every alarm in the house sounds simultaneously, alerting everyone instantly.
  • Constant Power: Hardwired directly into your home's electrical grid, so they are always on. They also feature 10-year sealed battery backups to keep them functional during grid outages.
  • Carbon Monoxide (CO) Integration: We install dual-sensor units or dedicated CO detectors near bedrooms and living areas to protect against the "silent killer" produced by gas appliances.

Maryland & D.C. Code Compliance

Maryland law specifically mandates that all homes be equipped with sealed, 10-year battery-backup smoke alarms. Furthermore, the NEC requires hardwired, interconnected smoke alarms in every sleeping room, outside each separate sleeping area, and on every level of the home. Eminent Power Solutions ensures your home meets or exceeds these critical life-safety codes.
