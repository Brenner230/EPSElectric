Reverse polarity means the hot and neutral wires were wired backward on the receptacle. While a lamp might still turn on, it creates a severe shock hazard because the exterior metal parts of appliances (like the shell of a toaster) can become electrified. We can quickly test and correct this.

It depends on the scope of work. Swapping out a few ungrounded outlets for GFCIs or fixing a double-tapped breaker usually does not require a permit. However, if the inspector flagged an unpermitted sub-panel or an illegal basement wire job, a retroactive permit and county inspection will be required to make it legal.

Federal Pacific Electric (FPE) and Zinsco panels have well-documented manufacturing defects where the breakers fail to trip during an overload, leading directly to fires. Because of this extreme liability, almost all homeowner's insurance companies will refuse to insure the home until the panel is fully replaced by a professional.
