Electrical problems are rarely as simple as they appear. A dead outlet in your bedroom might be caused by a loose neutral wire in the kitchen, or a failing breaker in the basement. Tripping Breakers

If resetting a breaker becomes a weekly chore, your circuit is either severely overloaded, or you have a dangerous short circuit hiding in your walls.

Flickering Lights

Often a sign of a loose wire connection, an overloaded neutral wire, or a failing main service drop from your local utility company.

Dead Outlets

When an entire room or wall goes dead, it usually indicates a broken continuous circuit, a tripped hidden GFCI, or a melted wire nut.

Burning Smells

This is a severe emergency. If you smell burning plastic near an outlet or panel, turn off the main breaker immediately and call a professional.
