The Danger Hiding in Your Walls

Between 1965 and 1973, single-strand aluminum wiring was frequently used in residential construction across the DMV due to a national copper shortage. Today, the Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) reports that homes with aluminum wiring are 55 times more likely to experience a catastrophic fire hazard than homes wired with copper. Hover or tap the cards below to understand why it fails.

Thermal Expansion

Aluminum expands and contracts significantly more than copper as electricity flows. This constant movement eventually loosens connections, causing arcing and intense heat.

Oxidation Resistance

When aluminum rusts (oxidizes), the outer layer acts as an insulator rather than a conductor, forcing electricity to push harder, generating fire-starting heat.

Galvanic Action

Standard modern outlets are designed for copper. When aluminum touches these brass/copper components, a corrosive chemical reaction destroys the connection.

Uninsurable Homes

Due to the massive liability, most major home insurance companies will refuse to bind a policy on a home with untreated aluminum wiring, stalling real estate sales.

Approved Remediation Solutions

While a full home rewire is the ultimate solution, it is highly invasive and expensive. Eminent Power Solutions offers a much more affordable, code-compliant alternative. We use specialized AlumiConn® connectors to "pigtail" a short piece of copper wire to the end of your existing aluminum wires. This creates a safe, permanent copper connection to your modern outlets and switches, eliminating the fire risk at the terminal points without tearing down your walls.

The EPS Master-Level Process

  • Complete Device Remediation: We open every single switch, outlet, and light fixture junction box in the home to address all vulnerable connection points.
  • Anti-Oxidant Paste: We coat the bare aluminum wires with a specialized anti-oxidant compound to prevent future degradation before securing them in the AlumiConn lug.
  • Torque Precision: Every connection is tightened to exact factory specifications using a calibrated torque screwdriver—a critical step that untrained handymen miss.
  • Panel Inspection: We evaluate your main electrical panel to ensure the heavy-gauge aluminum feeder wires are properly torqued and coated, preventing a catastrophic panel fire.
