The Problem with Aluminum Wiring

Between 1965 and 1973, single-strand aluminum wiring was frequently used in residential construction across the DMV due to a copper shortage. Today, the Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) reports that homes with aluminum wiring are 55 times more likely to experience "fire hazard conditions" than homes wired with copper.

Why Does It Fail?

  • Thermal Expansion: Aluminum expands and contracts more than copper when current flows through it. Over time, this loosens the connections at outlets and switches, creating arcing and intense heat.
  • Oxidation: When aluminum rusts (oxidizes), the rusted layer acts as an insulator rather than a conductor, forcing electricity to push through harder and generating extreme heat.
  • Galvanic Action: Standard modern outlets are made for copper. When aluminum touches these copper components, it causes a corrosive chemical reaction.

Approved Remediation Solutions

If a full home rewire is outside your budget, Eminent Power Solutions offers safe, code-compliant remediation. We use specialized AlumiConn® connectors to "pigtail" a short piece of copper wire to the end of the existing aluminum wire. This creates a safe, permanent copper connection to your modern outlets and switches, eliminating the fire risk at the terminal points.
