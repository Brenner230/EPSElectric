Stop Overloading Your Breakers

If running the microwave and the toaster at the same time causes the lights to go out, you are overloading a shared circuit. Heavy-draw appliances require their own "dedicated" lane of electricity straight from the breaker panel to function safely and efficiently in your DMV home. Hover or tap the cards below to see common dedicated circuits we install.

Induction Ranges

Converting from gas? Modern induction cooktops require massive power, usually needing a dedicated 240V, 40 to 50-amp circuit installed.

HVAC & Heat Pumps

Central air compressors and modern electric heat pumps require heavy-gauge wire and dedicated breakers to handle their massive startup surges.

Washers & Dryers

Electric dryers pull immense amounts of current. A dedicated 30-amp circuit ensures your laundry room operates safely without melting wires.

Sump Pumps

Never put a sump pump on a shared circuit. If another appliance trips the breaker during a storm, your unpowered pump will lead to a flooded basement.

Transitioning from Gas to Electric

One of the most common requests we receive is from homeowners swapping their old gas ranges for high-efficiency electric appliances. A gas stove only needs a standard 120V outlet to spark the igniter, but an electric stove needs a massive 240V supply. We expertly snake this heavy-gauge wire from your kitchen directly to your main panel, upgrading your capacity safely without destroying your walls.

The EPS Master-Level Process

  • Strict Load Calculations: Before installing a new 50-amp dedicated circuit, we verify that your home's main electrical panel has the capacity to support it without overloading the main breaker.
  • Safe Wire Routing: We fish new, heavy-gauge copper wiring through your walls, ceilings, or crawlspaces to the exact location of your new appliance, minimizing any need for drywall repair.
  • Code-Mandated Protection: We install the correct type of breaker for the appliance—including dual-function AFCI/GFCI breakers as required by the latest National Electrical Code (NEC) for modern kitchens and laundry rooms.
  • Panel Upgrades: If your older home lacks the physical space or amperage capacity for the new appliance, we can seamlessly bundle the circuit installation with a panel heavy-up.

Dedicated Circuit FAQs

A dedicated circuit is an electrical line that has its own breaker in your electrical box and serves only a single appliance or outlet. This ensures that heavy-drawing appliances don't compete for electricity, which prevents tripped breakers and electrical fires.

As a general rule, any appliance with a motor or heating element (refrigerators, microwaves, ovens, space heaters, hot tubs) requires a dedicated circuit. The National Electrical Code requires dedicated circuits for major permanent appliances to prevent system overloads.

If your panel is out of physical space, we have a few options. If your panel has the total amperage capacity, we might be able to install "tandem" breakers to condense existing circuits and free up space. If your panel is both full and maxed out on amperage, a panel upgrade or a sub-panel installation will be required.
