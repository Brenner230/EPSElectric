Circuits

Power your heavy-duty appliances safely. We install dedicated 120V and 240V circuits to protect your home's grid from overloads and tripping breakers.

Stop Overloading Your Breakers

If running the microwave and the toaster at the same time causes the lights to go out, you are overloading a shared circuit. Heavy-draw appliances require their own "dedicated" lane of electricity straight from the breaker panel to function safely and efficiently.

Transitioning from Gas to Induction

One of the most common requests we receive across the DMV is from homeowners swapping out their old gas ranges for modern, high-efficiency induction cooktops. A gas stove only needs a standard 120V outlet to spark the igniter. An induction stove requires a dedicated 240-volt, 40 or 50-amp circuit. We expertly snake this heavy-gauge wire from your kitchen back to your main panel, upgrading your capacity safely.

Appliances That Require Dedicated Lines:

  • Kitchen Powerhouses: Induction ranges, double wall ovens, built-in microwaves, and commercial-grade refrigerators.
  • HVAC & Heating: Central air conditioning compressors, heat pumps, and electric furnaces.
  • Utility & Basement: Washers, electric dryers, deep freezers, and sump pumps (to prevent a tripped breaker from causing a flooded basement).

Note: If you are adding multiple high-draw appliances during a kitchen remodel, we will perform a full load calculation to determine if your home requires a panel heavy-up.
