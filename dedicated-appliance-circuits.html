One Appliance, One Line

Sharing a circuit between high-draw appliances and lights leads to voltage sag and fire hazards. A Dedicated Circuit is a private electrical lane from your panel to a single device, ensuring it receives the specific amperage required for peak performance.

Induction Ranges

HVAC Systems

AC compressors pull massive "inrush" current; dedicated lines prevent the lights from dimming when the cooling kicks on.

Double Ovens

Sump Pumps

Warning Signs of System Burden

  • Frequent Trips: Breakers clicking off when you use two items at once.
  • Voltage Sag: Lights dimming significantly when the fridge or AC kicks on.
  • Buzzing/Heat: Warm outlet covers indicate wires are overheating behind the wall.
  • Premature Failure: Inconsistent voltage wears out appliance motors 50% faster.
