Bethesda, MD Panel Heavy-Up EV Charger

200-Amp Heavy-Up & EV Integration

This 1950s colonial was running on an original 100-amp split-bus panel that was completely maxed out. When the homeowner purchased a new EV, their system couldn't safely handle the load. We coordinated with Pepco for a total service drop disconnection, rebuilt the meter base, and installed a modern 200-amp load center. We then pulled a dedicated 60-amp copper circuit through the basement joists to hardwire a Level 2 Wall Connector in the garage.

  • Utility coordination and complete 200A service upgrade.
  • Removal of fire-hazard double-tapped breakers.
  • Hardwired Level 2 EV Charging Station installation.
  • Passed Montgomery County electrical inspection on first review.

Capitol Hill, D.C. Historic Wiring Code Correction

Historic Knob & Tube Remediation

The buyers of this beautiful 1890s rowhouse could not secure homeowner's insurance due to active knob-and-tube (K&T) wiring. Preserving the historic plaster ceilings was paramount. Using specialized endoscopic cameras and fiberglass fish tape, we strategically routed modern, grounded Romex (NM-B) cabling from the basement panel to the second floor, abandoning the brittle K&T in place without destroying the home's architecture.

  • Circuit tracing and separation of historic vs. modern wiring.
  • Surgical wire fishing to preserve original plaster and lath.
  • Upgrading ungrounded 2-prong outlets to NEC-compliant GFCIs.
  • Provided certified documentation to satisfy the insurance underwriter.

Columbia, MD Fire Prevention Aluminum Repair

Whole-Home Aluminum Wiring Remediation

Built in 1971, this home featured single-strand aluminum wiring, presenting a severe fire hazard due to thermal expansion at the outlet terminals. A complete tear-out was cost-prohibitive. We executed a CPSC-approved remediation by opening over 80 individual receptacles and switch boxes. We applied specialized anti-oxidant paste and used AlumiConn® connectors to permanently "pigtail" copper wiring to the aluminum, restoring total safety to the home while upgrading the aesthetics to modern Decora switches.

  • Systematic opening and inspection of all branch circuit devices.
  • Application of anti-oxidant compounds to prevent wire corrosion.
  • Precision-torqued AlumiConn® copper pigtail installations.
  • Replacement of failing receptacles with modern, tamper-resistant outlets.
