When Do You Need an Inspection?

We highly recommend booking an inspection if your home is over 40 years old, if you've recently experienced a major storm or lightning strike, before you install heavy appliances (like an EV charger), or if you are preparing to buy/sell a property to avoid closing delays.

Diagnostic Audit FAQs

A general home inspector is a generalist. They test a few outlets but do not perform load calculations or remove dead-fronts to check busbars. A Master Electrician utilizes specialized meters to check ground resistance and voltage drops that an inspector will miss.

The Electrical Safety Foundation recommends a thorough inspection every 10 years for modern homes, and every 3 to 5 years for homes older than 40 years, especially those with legacy wiring like aluminum.

Our primary goal is diagnosis and reporting. However, if we uncover a minor, immediate life-safety hazard (like a loose wire sparking in the panel), we can often execute a rapid repair on the spot with your approval.
