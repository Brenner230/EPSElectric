Peace of Mind Behind the Walls

The majority of your home's electrical system is completely hidden from view. While a general home inspector might test a few outlets, they do not perform a deep-dive analysis of your panel's load capacity, wiring integrity, or grounding system. An audit by a licensed Master Electrician is the only way to guarantee your home is truly safe. Hover or tap below.

Panel Integrity

We inspect the main breaker box for heat damage, internal oxidation, illegal double-taps, and recalled components that pose fire risks.

Grounding & Bonding

We verify your home has a clear, low-resistance path to the earth to protect your family and appliances during a lightning strike or surge.

Hidden Wiring

We check attics and crawlspaces for rodent damage, improper open-air splices, and obsolete K&T or aluminum wiring.

Code Violations

We test every GFCI and AFCI outlet for proper trip speed and identify unpermitted DIY work that could stall a home sale.

When Do You Need an Inspection?

We highly recommend booking an inspection if your home is over 40 years old, if you've recently experienced a major storm or lightning strike, before you install heavy appliances (like an EV charger), or if you are preparing to buy/sell a property to avoid closing delays.

The EPS Master-Level Process

  • Systematic Testing: We methodically work our way from the exterior meter base, through the main panel, and into every branch circuit of the home using calibrated, commercial-grade testing equipment.
  • Detailed Reporting: After the inspection, we don't just give you a verbal summary. We provide a structured report outlining immediate safety hazards versus suggested, modern upgrades.
  • Transparent Estimates: If we uncover any code violations or failing components, we provide an upfront, no-pressure estimate for the necessary code corrections so you know exactly what is required to make the home safe.

Safety Inspection FAQs

A general home inspector looks at everything from the roof to the plumbing, but they are not licensed electricians. They typically only test a few outlets and look visually at the panel. A master electrician performs load calculations, removes the panel cover to inspect busbar health, and utilizes specialized meters to check ground resistance and voltage drops that an inspector will miss.

The Electrical Safety Foundation recommends a thorough inspection every 10 years for modern homes, and every 3 to 5 years for homes older than 40 years (especially those with aluminum wiring, which requires frequent checking of connection points).
