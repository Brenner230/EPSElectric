Peace of Mind Behind the Walls

The majority of your home's electrical system is hidden from view. While a general home inspector might test a few outlets, they do not perform a deep-dive analysis of your panel's load capacity, wiring integrity, or grounding system. An audit by a licensed Master Electrician is the only way to guarantee your home is truly safe.

When Do You Need an Inspection?

  • Buying or Selling a Home: Ensure there are no unpermitted "handyman" additions that could stall your closing or void your insurance.
  • Homes Over 40 Years Old: Older DMV homes often hide degraded insulation, ungrounded circuits, or obsolete aluminum and knob-and-tube wiring.
  • After a Major Storm or Surge: Lightning strikes can cause micro-damage to wiring insulation that isn't immediately apparent but poses a severe latent fire risk.
  • Adding Heavy Appliances: Before installing a hot tub or EV charger, you must verify your panel can handle the load safely.

What We Check:

Our comprehensive audit includes testing the main service panel for oxidation and double-tapped breakers, verifying proper grounding/bonding, testing GFCI/AFCI functionality, and inspecting visible wiring for code violations.
