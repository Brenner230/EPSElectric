GFCI: Shock Protection

Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters (GFCI) constantly monitor the flow of electricity. If they detect even a tiny amount of current leaking to the ground (like through water or a person), they cut power in 1/40th of a second.

Required in: Kitchens, bathrooms, garages, outdoors, and basements.

AFCI: Fire Protection

Arc Fault Circuit Interrupters (AFCI) detect dangerous sparking (arcing) behind your walls caused by damaged wires, loose connections, or pierced insulation before it can ignite the wood framing.

Required in: Bedrooms, living rooms, hallways, and dining areas.

Fixing "No Equipment Ground"

If your older home has two-prong outlets, simply swapping them for three-prong outlets is illegal and dangerous. We can safely install GFCI receptacles at the beginning of the circuit to legally provide three-prong convenience while delivering shock protection, even without a true ground wire.
