Upgrading Your Home's Receptacles

Standard two-prong or basic three-prong outlets do not actively protect you from electrical faults. If your home was built before the 2000s, it likely lacks the specialized, active protection required by modern code. GFCI & AFCI Outlet Installation ensures your home actively monitors for dangerous leaks and sparks.

GFCI: Shock Defense

GFCI units cut power in 1/40th of a second if they sense electricity leaking to the ground, preventing severe electrocution.

AFCI: Fire Defense

AFCI units detect dangerous micro-sparking behind your walls caused by damaged wires before they can start a fire.

GFCI Locations

The NEC strictly requires GFCI protection in all "wet" areas: Kitchens, bathrooms, garages, and exteriors.

AFCI Locations

Modern code requires AFCI protection in almost all living spaces, including bedrooms, living rooms, and hallways.

Device Safety Specifications

Ground Fault Circuit Interrupters

GFCI devices monitor the current going out vs. coming back. If there is a mismatch of even 5 milliamps, it instantly cuts power to save your life.

5mA

Micro-Leak Detection

Trips instantly at a 5-milliamp current differential.

1/40s

Trip Speed

Cuts power in 25 milliseconds to prevent lethal heart fibrillation.

WR

Weather Resistant

Specialized WR-rated devices for UV and rain exposed exterior outlets.

TEST

Self-Testing

Modern units run internal auto-diagnostics to verify sensor health.

LOAD

Downstream Protection

Wiring the "Load" terminals to protect standard outlets down the line.

TR

Tamper Resistant

Internal shutters block foreign objects to protect children.

LED

Status Indicators

Visual light confirmation when the unit has tripped or failed.

DUAL

Dual-Function

Combination GFCI/AFCI devices for overlapping safety zones.

Arc Fault Circuit Interrupters

AFCI technology relies on advanced microprocessors to distinguish between normal motor arcing (like a vacuum) and dangerous wiring arcing.

SERIES

Series Arc Detection

Detects sparking along a single broken or degraded wire.

PARA

Parallel Arc Detection

Detects sparking between two adjacent compromised wires.

CHIP

Microprocessor Logic

Advanced algorithms prevent nuisance tripping from vacuums.

PANEL

Breaker Installation

Panel-mounted AFCI breakers protect the entire branch circuit.

HEAT

Thermal Prevention

Stops electrical fires inside walls before the wire sheath melts.

CODE

Bedroom Mandates

Strictly required for all bedroom outlets to protect sleepers.

PIG

Neutral Pigtails

Properly landing neutral coils to the busbar for AFCI function.

DIAG

Fault Diagnostics

Retrieving trip-codes from smart breakers to trace the fault source.

The Two-Prong Dilemma

  • The Danger: If your older home has two-prong outlets, simply swapping them for standard three-prong outlets is illegal and dangerous because there is no true earth ground to protect you.
  • The Solution: We can safely install GFCI receptacles at the beginning of the circuit to legally provide three-prong convenience while delivering massive shock protection.
