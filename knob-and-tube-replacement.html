The Hidden Danger in Historic Homes

Knob and tube (K&T) wiring was the standard electrical installation method from the 1880s through the 1940s. While it was state-of-the-art for its time, it presents severe hazards for the modern homeowner in historic districts like Downtown Frederick or Capitol Hill.

Why K&T Must Be Replaced

  • No Ground Wire: K&T systems only have a hot and neutral wire. Without a ground, electrical faults can shock you or destroy expensive appliances rather than safely tripping a breaker.
  • Degraded Insulation: The original rubber and cloth insulation around these wires becomes brittle over decades, flaking off and leaving bare, live wires exposed inside your walls.
  • Overheating Risks: K&T wires were designed to be suspended in open air to dissipate heat. When modern homeowners blow insulation over K&T wiring in their attics, it traps the heat and creates a massive fire hazard.
  • Insurance & Mortgages: Most major home insurance companies will flat-out refuse to insure a property with active K&T wiring, making it nearly impossible to buy or sell the home.

Minimally Invasive Rewiring

We respect the architectural integrity of your historic home. Eminent Power Solutions uses specialized snake-and-fish techniques to route modern, grounded Romex wiring behind original plaster and lath, keeping wall patching to an absolute minimum.
