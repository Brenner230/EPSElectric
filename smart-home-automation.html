The "Neutral Wire" Problem in Older Homes

If you've tried to install a smart switch in a historic D.C. or Frederick home, you likely discovered a missing white wire. Modern smart switches require a "neutral" wire to maintain continuous power for their Wi-Fi chips, even when the light is off. Our electricians can safely retrofit your older switch boxes to accommodate modern smart technology.

A standard light switch simply breaks the circuit to turn a light off. But a smart switch has a built-in computer (Wi-Fi or Zigbee chip) that needs power 24/7 to listen for commands from your phone. The neutral wire completes a small, continuous circuit to power the chip even when the main light bulb is turned off.

A smart panel (like SPAN) replaces your standard breaker box. It connects to your phone and allows you to view the real-time energy consumption of every single circuit in your house. It allows you to remotely turn off breakers and is incredibly valuable for managing backup battery storage or solar power during an outage.
