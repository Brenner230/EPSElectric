The "Neutral Wire" Problem in Older Homes

  • The Issue: If you've tried to install a smart switch in a historic DMV home, you likely discovered a missing white wire. Smart switches need a "neutral" to power their Wi-Fi chips when the light is off.
  • The Fix: Our electricians can safely retrofit your older switch boxes, fishing new neutral lines through the walls to perfectly accommodate modern automation hardware.

Battery-powered cameras "sleep" to save power, missing the first crucial seconds of an event. Hardwired cameras are always on, record continuously, utilize brighter floodlights, and never require you to climb a ladder to swap a battery.

A smart panel (like SPAN) replaces your standard breaker box. It connects to an app to show the real-time energy consumption of every circuit in your house. It allows you to remotely turn off breakers and is vital for managing backup battery storage during an outage.

Most video doorbells (like Ring Pro or Nest) require a 16V to 24V transformer to maintain power. Many older homes still have 10V transformers for basic chimes. We can locate and upgrade your hidden transformer to provide the proper voltage to the smart doorbell.
