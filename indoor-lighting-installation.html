Lighting & Automation FAQs

We use modern "canless" LED wafers that do not require bulky metal housing. This allows us to cut a precise hole in the exact spot you want the light and fish the wire through the void. While small access holes are occasionally needed in complex layouts, we always strive for a damage-free install.

Yes! Many older homes in the DMV rely entirely on switch-controlled wall outlets. We can tap into an existing power source, run wiring up into the ceiling, and install a brand-new overhead lighting grid controlled by a new wall switch.

Color temperature is measured in Kelvins (K) and heavily dictates the mood of a room. Most LEDs we install have a selectable switch so you can dial in the perfect warmth.

No. Both the LED fixture itself AND the wall switch must be rated as "Dimmable." Furthermore, the switch must be an "LED-compatible" dimmer. Mixing old incandescent dimmers with modern LEDs causes severe flickering, buzzing, and premature bulb failure.

CRI measures how accurately a light source shows true colors (scale of 0-100). Cheap LEDs have a low CRI, making food and skin tones look dull. We strictly install high-CRI LEDs (90+) to make your home's colors pop vividly.
